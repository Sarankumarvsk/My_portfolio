INTRODUCTION

The automatic dam shutter system utilizes an ESP32 microcontroller to manage inputs from a float switch and an ultrasonic sensor, along with LED indicators for operational status. The float switch detects water levels, closing when low and opening when high, while the ultrasonic sensor provides real-time measurements of the water surface. LEDs are used to signal system status: green for normal operation, yellow for caution, and red for high water levels. Within the main loop, the system continuously checks readings from both sensors. If the ultrasonic sensor detects high water levels, it triggers the float switch and activates the red LED. The dam shutter is controlled via a relay or motor driver based on the float switch state, and caution alerts are issued with the yellow LED if water levels approach a warning threshold. In emergencies, the system automatically closes the dam shutter and activates an alarm. Optionally, data can be logged to a cloud service for monitoring and alerting through a mobile app or web dashboard, ensuring effective water level management and safety.
